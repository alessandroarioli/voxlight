Aurora

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

An aurora, also commonly known as the northern lights or southern lights, is a natural light display in Earth's sky. Auroras are the result of disturbances in the Earth's magnetosphere caused by the solar wind.

Major disturbances result from enhancements in the speed of the solar wind from coronal holes and coronal mass ejections. These disturbances alter the trajectories of charged particles in the magnetospheric plasma.

These particles, mainly electrons and protons, precipitate into the upper atmosphere. The resulting ionization and excitation of atmospheric constituents emit light of varying colour and complexity.
